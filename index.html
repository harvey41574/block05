Bears

Large, omniverous mammals native to most continents of the world. Bears are beloved in pop culture, but can be dangerous, especially as encounters with humans are becoming more commonplace as their habitats are becoming more threatened. Their are many different species of these majestic beasts throughout the world.

Giraffes

Known for their long necks, Giraffes are herbivores native to Africa. They use their necks to access the tall branches to feed on. These giants are the tallest animal on Earth.

Lions

The "King of the Jungle" actually lives on the plains of Africa. These large, carniverous cats are known for the thick mane around the males necks, although it is the females that do most of the work hunting. They live in family groups known as prides.

Monkeys

These clever, agile omnivores are found throughout the world, mostly in tropical rainforests. Monkeys are primates that live in family groups. There are around 160 different species, ranging in size from small enough to fit in your palm to the size of large dog.

Alligators

The largest carniverous reptile in North America is part of the Crocodile family. Alligators can primarily be seen in the swamps of Loiusiana and Florida. They can be aggressive and dangerous, with razor sharp teeth and long tails that they use as weapons. They can grow to 15 feet and weigh as much as 1,000 lbs.
